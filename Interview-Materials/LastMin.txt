1️⃣ Cloud Fundamentals

Explain IaaS vs PaaS vs SaaS through real trade-offs, not definitions. When would you recommend public vs private vs hybrid cloud and why? Show your reasoning, not just vocabulary.

2️⃣ AWS Core Services

Know EC2, S3, RDS, Lambda, VPC - but more importantly, explain how they connect to solve problems. Can you trace a request flowing from user through VPC, hitting EC2 or Lambda, reading from RDS, and serving from S3?

3️⃣ Cloud Architecture & Design

Don't just mention the Well-Architected Framework - apply it. When discussing high availability or disaster recovery, explain your reasoning: What's your RTO and RPO? How does auto-scaling interact with load balancing? The WHY matters more than the WHAT.

4️⃣ Security & Compliance

Show you understand defense in depth: Security Groups vs NACLs? Encryption at rest and in transit? How CloudTrail fits into auditing? AWS Shield vs WAF? Explain IAM least privilege clearly - it's table stakes.

5️⃣ DevOps & Automation

Explain how you'd deploy infrastructure repeatedly with IaC (CloudFormation/Terraform), CI/CD pipelines, and containers (ECS/EKS). Discuss trade-offs: ECS vs EKS? Lambda vs containers? Your decisions reveal how you think.

6️⃣ Cost Optimization & Troubleshooting

Prove you can operate in the real world. Anyone can architect a solution that works - can you make it affordable? Discuss Reserved Instances vs Savings Plans, right-sizing, CloudWatch alarms, and real constraints you've navigated.

7️⃣ Behavioral Questions

Use STAR method but lead with business impact, not technology. "I reduced deployment failures by 60% through automated testing" beats "I worked with CodePipeline."

Technical knowledge starts the conversation. Judgment and reasoning get you the offer.
